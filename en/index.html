Philosophy

Performance is physiological.

In high-cognitive-load professions, your recovery capacity directly conditions your lucidity. Properly programmed exercise is the most underestimated lever.

Observation 01

Failing recovery

Physiological depletion alters judgement long before fatigue is consciously felt. Low HRV, elevated RHR, fragmented sleep: the signals exist before the symptoms.

Observation 02

The time available

Your schedule is your primary constraint. My protocol fits within it without overloading it, by maximising the physiological gain of every minute of training.

The Approach

A protocol grounded in physiology

My expertise is focused on training as a physiological tool. No nutrition, no life coaching — a precise scope, a scientific rigour.

Programming

Periodisation of effort

Strength, conditioning and mobility cycles calibrated to your professional load. A focus on long-term progression and adherence, not on immediate intensity.

Physiology & Biomechanics

Functional assessment

Objective measurements: maximal strength, aerobic capacities (VO₂, thresholds), grip test, body composition. A scientific foundation for optimising physical capacity in service of cognitive performance.

Monitoring

Longitudinal analysis

Tracking internal load (HRV, RHR) via connected devices (Polar H10, Oura, Whoop, Garmin). Weekly adjustments based on your recovery signals.

About

The human behind the method

In 2017, between preparatory classes and financial audit at KPMG, my own system gave way. The burnout delayed my graduation. It was also a turning point.

I learnt the hard way that good decisions are not made within an exhausted body. Performance does not lie in the accumulation of hours, but in the physiological infrastructure that carries us.

Over 7+ years I have worked with 70+ individual clients — university professors at top engineering schools, executives, researchers, high-level amateur athletes — and led collective programmes: Volleyball at Nationale 3 level, functional sessions of up to 30 people, and technical supervision for groups of 60+ participants.

Today I hold two complementary roles: Strength & Conditioning Coach on the field, and Human Performance Strategist in the design of hybrid strategies — health, physical, cognitive.
