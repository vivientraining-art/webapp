Frequently asked

What companies ask

Do participants need to be athletic?
No. Sessions are accessible to all levels: intensity is individualised through heart-rate zones, so everyone works at their own threshold. The goal isn't athletic performance, but physiological adaptation.
How much time does it take?
One 1-hour session per week for the programme, or a single 1h30 session for the workshop. The protocol is designed to fit a saturated schedule without overloading it.
What happens to our employees' heart-rate data?
Measurements are stored locally on the tablet — nothing is sent to a third-party server — and can be deleted at any time. Individual results are shared only with the person concerned; at company level, only aggregated, anonymised trends are reported.
What equipment and space are needed?
I bring all the equipment (Polar H10 sensors, dynamometer, iPad). A meeting room or an outdoor space is enough; your teams simply come in comfortable clothing.
What if our teams are multi-site or remote?
That's covered. Tracking can run remotely via connected devices (Polar, Oura, Whoop, Garmin) and shared monitoring. I work on-site around Paris / Saclay, and remotely elsewhere.
How is the result measured?
Through a data assessment at the start and end of the programme, on markers agreed upfront (resting HR, variability, strength, cognitive reaction time). You leave with a quantified report — not an impression.
